The Work

Most of the time, the tech
isn't the problem

Nine out of ten "technical" crises I've walked into were ownership problems wearing an architecture costume. No one decided who was accountable. Roadmaps kept getting reprioritised. Engineers kept rewriting the same module because the spec kept changing. I don't come in and generate a report. I sit with the actual constraint - whether that's a codebase, a process, or a team dynamic - and work out what has to change and in what order. The output is always a decision, not a deck.

Who Books These Sessions

You're probably here because
something isn't working

Most people who reach out aren't looking for a vendor. They're looking for someone who's been in the room where that exact decision got made - and can tell them what they'd do differently with the benefit of hindsight. That's the conversation this session is for.

CEO
Scaling past product-market fit but engineering has stalled
The team is busy. Standups are full. Nothing ships. You know something's wrong but everyone has a different explanation. You need one person to cut through and tell you what's actually broken.
CEO
Inherited a technology function you didn't build
You took over a company or a division. The tech is a black box. The previous CTO is gone. You need to understand what you're sitting on - and whether the person running it is the right one for the next phase.
CEO
Platform is breaking under growth
What worked at 10,000 users is falling apart at 500,000. Your team is firefighting instead of building. You need a structural answer, not another sprint of hotfixes.
CEO
Evaluating AI investment but unsure what's real
Everyone is pitching AI. You want to know which parts of your business actually benefit from it, what it genuinely costs to implement, and what your team needs to be able to do before it makes sense.
Founder
No senior technical voice at the table
You have engineers, but no one in the room who's made architecture decisions at scale. Choices that look fine today are quietly becoming expensive. You need a second opinion from someone who's seen where this goes.
Founder

FAQ

Quick answers

Is the session free?
Yes. Thirty minutes, no charge, no pitch. I'd rather spend half an hour figuring out whether I can genuinely help before either of us commits to anything more.
What should I prepare before the call?
One problem. Not a deck, not a background document. Just come with the thing that's actually keeping you up at night. The more specific you are, the more useful the conversation will be.

Yes. If the organisation and the mandate are right, it's worth talking. Book a slot - we'll know in thirty minutes whether there's something worth taking further.
